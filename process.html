Process

Working agreements are structured in three independent but correlated phases.

Phase 1

I begin by arranging a time to drop in for a tour and to meet all the stakeholders in the project. I guide the team through a process to dig deep and clearly define the goals and objectives for their project — while closely considering budgets and timelines.

After our meeting, I compile recommendations about how to move forward with the project.

Phase 2

After reviewing my recommendations, we meet again to answer any questions

For many years, I was on the receiving-end of countless RFPs.

Here’s a secret: The best team for a project probably won’t even respond to an RFP. Moreover, unless an RFP is concise, the vendors that do respond will base their bids on vague interpretations. Organizations subject themselves to tremendous risk by signing agreements before they actually know — for sure — what they’re getting in to.
